Mathura,Uttar Pradesh

Mathura is revered as the birthplace of Lord Krishna, one of the most beloved and worshipped deities in Hinduism. It is believed that Lord Krishna was born in Mathura in the Dwapara Yuga (an era in Hindu mythology) to Queen Devaki and King Vasudeva.

Mathura is closely associated with the childhood and early life of Lord Krishna. The city is believed to be the setting for many of Krishna's divine and playful activities, including his famous Rasleela (a divine dance with his devotees) and various other pastimes. The Krishna Janmabhoomi is the main religious site in Mathura, identified as the place of Janmasthan Temple is a significant pilgrimage site for devotees, attracting millions of visitors each year. Mathura is dotted with numerous temples dedicated to Lord Krishna and other deities. The Dwarkadhish Temple, Banke Bihari Temple, and Prem Mandir are among the prominent ones. The city also has several ghats (steps leading to the river) along the Yamuna River, where devotees perform rituals and take holy dips. Mathura is known for its grand celebrations of various Hindu festivals, especially Krishna Janmashtami. This festival marks Lord Krishna's birth and is observed with great enthusiasm in Mathura, with colorful processions, devotional singing, and reenactments of Krishna's life events

Mathura has a rich cultural and historical heritage. The city has been a center of art, architecture, and learning for centuries. It is home to ancient temples, ruins, and archaeological sites that showcase the artistic and architectural brilliance of the region.
